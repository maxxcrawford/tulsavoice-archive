GALLERY: Tulsa Marches with Standing Rock

March from BOK Center to Bank of America on Nov. 25

By Liz Blood, photos by Joseph Rushmore


March organizer Olivia Ramirez leads the march east down W. 3rd St.

Joseph Rushmore

On Friday, Nov. 25, hundreds of Tulsans gathered outside the BOK Center in downtown Tulsa for "Tulsa Marches with Standing Rock," a rally and march to the Bank of America Financial Center at the corner of W. 6th St. and S. Boulder Ave. Bank of America is one of the institutions supporting the Dakota Access Pipeline. Self-ascribed water protectors have urged Americans across the country to pull their money out of banks supporting DAPL. At this gathering, a handful of Tulsans did so. Others sat in the lobby and faced arrests, though none were made. The march occurred the same day the Army Corps of Engineers issued an eviction notice to the Oceti Sakowin camp at Standing Rock—and any areas near the Cannonball River that are federally-managed property. The eviction goes into effect Monday, Dec. 5. Yesterday, Nov. 28, North Dakota governor Jack Dalrymple issued an emergency evacuation of the camp due to harsh winter conditions.
